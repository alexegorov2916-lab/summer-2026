Summer 2026: Dolomiti & Levanto Road Trip

✎ Prague → Dolomiti → Levanto → home again

🌎 Interesting Facts About the Countries We Passed

  • Czech Republic: Home to over 2,000 castles — one of the highest densities in the world.
  • Austria: More than 60% of the country is covered by the Alps.
  • Italy: Has more UNESCO World Heritage Sites than any other country.
  • Germany: Its Autobahn network is one of the oldest and fastest highway systems in Europe.

🚜 Family Road Trip Story

We packed our car in Prague with way too many snacks and absolutely no idea how many tunnels we were about to drive through. The Dolomiti greeted us with towering cliffs, Levanto welcomed us with warm waves, and on the way back we zig-zagged through Austria and Germany like seasoned road-trip pros. By the end, our car looked like it had survived a small natural disaster — but the memories were worth every crumb, every detour, and every "Are we there yet?" moment.

Dolomiti Mountains

🏔 pale cliffs, alpine glow & a cycling race we never expected

⛰️ About the Dolomiti

The Dolomiti, also known as the Dolomite Mountains, are a mountain range in northeastern Italy and part of the Southern Limestone Alps. Their highest peak is Marmolada, reaching 3,343 meters. The region is famous for its dramatic pale cliffs, unique rock formations, and the phenomenon of "alpenglow," where the mountains turn pink at sunset.

🚴 Cycling Race Incident

Our peaceful mountain drive turned into an unexpected adventure when we suddenly found ourselves stuck behind a massive cycling race. Dozens of cyclists zoomed past us while we sat there, trapped on a narrow road with absolutely no chance to move. The kids started cheering like we were at the Tour de France, while we tried to figure out if we'd ever escape the mountains before sunset. Eventually the race passed, and we continued our journey — slightly delayed but with a great story to tell.

Levanto — Sea, Sun & Adventure

🏗️ sandy beaches, surf waves & a Medusa mission

🌊 About Levanto

Levanto is a charming seaside town on the Ligurian coast of Italy, known for its sandy beaches, surf-friendly waves, and access to the famous Cinque Terre trails. It blends relaxed coastal living with adventurous outdoor activities, making it a favorite spot for families and hikers alike.

🐝 Medusa Encounter

On our last day, we decided to take a boat trip — a perfect plan until we discovered the water was full of poisonous medusas. Suddenly swimming became a strategic mission: dodge left, dodge right, don't touch anything that wiggles. The kids treated it like an underwater obstacle course, while the adults tried to look brave. We made it back to the boat without a single sting, feeling like victorious sea explorers.
